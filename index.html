About

Research at the intersection of controls, buildings, and deployment.

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Purdue University, affiliated with Ray W. Herrick Laboratories. My research focuses on supervisory control and optimization for residential electrification, with an emphasis on systems that can work in real homes.

My work combines modeling, optimization, experimental deployment, field data, building systems, and grid-interactive control. I am especially interested in translating research into practical tools for homes, utilities, and energy technology companies.

Research Themes

Making electrified homes cheaper, smarter, and more responsive.

01

Cost-Effective Residential Electrification

Intelligent load coordination to help avoid unnecessary electrical service and panel upgrades while preserving comfort and service quality.

02

Predictive Control for Heat Pump Water Heaters

MPC and minimal-sensing control strategies for energy and cost savings under time-varying electricity prices and hot water constraints.

03

Supervisory HVAC Control

Comfort-aware and grid-aware supervisory control using predictive models, occupancy signals, and field demonstrations.

04

Grid-Flexible Homes

Home energy management methods that coordinate EVs, batteries, PV, water heating, and flexible loads under utility and grid needs.

05

DC Nanogrids and Vehicle-to-Home Systems

Experimental infrastructure for future home energy architectures, including EV integration, DC systems, hardware-in-the-loop testing, and controllable residential loads.

Whole-Home Current Limiting

Designed supervisory control strategies that coordinate major residential electric loads to keep total home current below panel limits, helping avoid costly electrical service upgrades.

  • Current constraints
  • Service upgrade avoidance
